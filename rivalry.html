Rivalry Timeline

1944

The game ended in a 13-13 tie after World War II forced the teams to schedule the game at the beginning of the season.

1952

With both teams undefeated, No. 3 UCLA fell to No. 4 USC 14-12.

1967

During what is known as the “Game of the Century”, No. 2 USC took down No. 1 UCLA 21-20.

1975

UCLA needed a win to reach the Rose Bowl and got it, defeating USC 25-20.

1976

No. 2 UCLA faced No. 3 USC, knowing the winner would advance to the Rose Bowl. USC came out on top, winning 24-14.

1988

With the winner earning a trip to the Rose Bowl, No. 2 USC beat No. 4 UCLA 31-22.

2005

Ranked No. 1 in the nation, USC walloped UCLA 66-19 and earned a bid to the BCS National Championship Game against Texas.

2006

A win would have sent No. 2 USC to the BCA National Championship, but the Bruins shocked the Trojans with a 13-9 upset.

2011

USC shutout UCLA with a 50-0 win, but the Bruins still won the Pac-12 South due to the sanctions on the Trojans.

2012

Playing in the rain, UCLA pays back USC for its loss the year before, winning 38-28 and going on to win the Pac-12 South.






the facts


  • Both schools are NCAA Division I
  • Separated by only 12 miles
  • How does the rivalry stay so active?
    The Trojan Knights (all men) and the Helenes (all women) at USC and the Rally Committee (coed) at UCLA are student organizations that keep the traditions and rivalry alive.
